║║║ i. Border tiles ╠══════════════════════╣
║║╠═════════════════╝                      ║
║║║ When designing the Super Gameboy tiles,║
║║║ there are some challenges to overcome. ║
║║║                                        ║
║║║ You get 4 palettes, 96 8x8 tiles,      ║
║║║ basic tile manipulation (flipping      ║
║║║ only, no rotation) and limited colours ║
║║║ (Red 0-31, Green 0-31, Blue 0-31).     ║
║║║                                        ║
║║║ With this in mind, new graphics were   ║
║║║ created to fit within the space the    ║
║║║ current tiles filled where possible.   ║
║║║ This was challenging as palettes also  ║
║║║ played a role in how the sprite could  ║
║║║ overlap other tiles where 2 palettes   ║
║║║ would clash. For instance, each sprite ║
║║║ is in front of a white circle with a   ║
║║║ thick blue outline. 4 tiles of which   ║
║║║ have to be blue, effectively cutting   ║
║║║ away 4 chunks from the four corners of ║
║║║ the circle. It's hard to explain.      ║
║║║                                        ║
║║║ In the end, the graphics went a little ║
║║║ over what the original Pokémon sprites ║
║║║ used, but its OK as there were some    ║
║║║ leftover tiles that weren't being used ║
║║║ (MNSTR!).                              ║
║║║                                        ║
║║║ Ken Sugimori's art for Clefairy,       ║
║║║ Vileplume and Horsea was used as a     ║
║║║ base for the spritework.               ║
║║║                                        ║
║║║ The palettes were tweaked carefully    ║
